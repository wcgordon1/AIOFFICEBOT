## How ATS Actually Reads Technical Resumes

### Beginner Explanation

An ATS scans your resume and extracts:
- Job titles
- Skills
- Dates
- Education
- Keywords
- Formatting structure

Then it compares those keywords against the job description.

If the system cannot parse your text properly, you are filtered out before a recruiter ever opens the file.

### Technical Depth

Modern ATS systems tokenize text, normalize keywords, and weight matches based on job description relevance. Poor structure lowers match scores. Misplaced headers break parsing logic. Non standard formatting causes segmentation errors.

If your resume says:

“Built scalable backend systems”

But the job says:

“Developed distributed backend services in Node.js and AWS”

You are missing keyword alignment.
