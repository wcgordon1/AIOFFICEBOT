## What Are Wedding Vow Cards

### Beginner Explanation

Wedding vow cards are small printed cards that contain your final vow text. You hold them during the ceremony instead of reading from your phone.

### Technical Depth

A properly formatted vow card includes:
- Clean line breaks
- Large readable font size
- Clear paragraph spacing
- Page breaks that avoid mid sentence cuts
- Balanced margins for handheld reading

Digital tools like [Free Wedding Vow Cards](https://www.vows.you/wedding-vow-cards) simplify formatting so you do not manually adjust Word or Google Docs.

## Formatting Best Practices

### Ideal Font Size

- Minimum 16 point
- 18 to 20 point recommended

### Line Spacing

- 1.3 to 1.5 line spacing
- Clear separation between promise sections

### Page Break Logic

Never break:
- Mid sentence
- Mid promise

If your vow is long, convert into:
- Card 1: Opening and story
- Card 2: Promises and close

## Glossary

### Line Break
Where text moves to a new line. Important for readability during live speaking.

### Margin
The white space around the text. Too small makes reading harder.

### Card Layout
Formatting text to fit within small printable pages held during ceremony.

## FAQs

### Should I use my phone instead of vow cards?

Phones can work, but screen lock, notifications, or glare create risk. Printed vow cards are more reliable.

### How many cards should I print?

At least two copies each. One for you, one backup.

### What paper should I use?

Medium to thick stock paper works best. Avoid glossy glare heavy finishes for outdoor weddings.
